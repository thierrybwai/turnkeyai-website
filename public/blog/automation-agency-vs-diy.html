The DIY Trap (Why It Fails)

Time Investment

What You Think: "I'll learn Zapier in a weekend"

Reality:

  • Learning Zapier properly: 40-80 hours ($1,400-$2,800 in your time)
  • Building first workflow: 8-16 hours
  • Debugging and fixing: 20-40 hours
  • Ongoing maintenance: 5-10 hours/month forever

Total first year: 100-150 hours of your time (3-4 months of part-time work)

Money Investment

What You Think: "Free tier is $0. Maybe $200/month."

Reality:

  • Learning tools: $0 (but your time = $1,400-$2,800)
  • Tool subscriptions: $200-$400/month
  • Integration fees: $50-$200/month
  • Fixing mistakes: $500-$2,000

Total first year: $3,000-$6,000+ (plus your time)

When DIY Actually Makes Sense

Go DIY if:

  • ✅ You're technical (developer, engineer)
  • ✅ You have time (4-6 months part-time)
  • ✅ Your processes are unique/specialized
  • ✅ You want to own the system

Do NOT go DIY if:

  • ❌ You're non-technical
  • ❌ You need it done quickly (weeks not months)
  • ❌ You want someone else managing it
  • ❌ Your time is worth $150-$300/hour
  • ❌ You can't afford the risk of automation breaking

What Good Agencies Do (That DIY Can't)

  • 🤝 Integration: Connect to any system
