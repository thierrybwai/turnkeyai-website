The DIY Trap (Why It Fails)

When DIY Actually Makes Sense

Go DIY if:

  • ✅ You're technical (developer, engineer, etc.)
  • ✅ You have time (can invest 4-6 months part-time)
  • ✅ Your processes are unique or specialized
  • ✅ You want to own the system
  • ✅ You're building tools for your business (not for customers)

Example: A tech founder automating their internal processes. They have time, they're technical, and they'll maintain it forever.

Do NOT go DIY if:

  • ❌ You're non-technical
  • ❌ You need it done quickly (weeks not months)
  • ❌ You want someone else managing it
  • ❌ Your time is more valuable as a business owner than as an automation engineer
  • ❌ You need 99.9% uptime and reliability

The Risk Factor

DIY Risk:

  • Automation fails in the middle of the night
  • You're on vacation when it breaks
  • Knowledge depends on one person
  • Vendor lock-in (if you use one tool heavily)
  • Technical debt (the more you build, the harder it is to maintain)

Agency Risk:

  • You're dependent on the agency
  • If they go out of business, you need a new one
  • Pricing could increase

Risk comparison: Agency risk is easier to mitigate (find a new agency). DIY risk is harder (you're the single point of failure).
